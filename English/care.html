Basic Rabbit Care

1. Providing a Healthy Diet and a Large Enclosure:

Ensure your rabbit has a balanced and nutritious diet, including hay, fresh vegetables, and pellets. Provide a spacious and safe living environment with hiding spots and comfortable flooring. Regularly check for any signs of health issues.

2. Rabbit-Proofing Your Home and Providing Toys and Social Enrichment:

Rabbit-proof your home by removing hazards and protecting electrical cords. Offer a variety of toys for mental stimulation, such as tunnels, balls, and puzzle feeders. Ensure social interaction by spending quality time with your rabbit and considering companionship if appropriate. Rotate and introduce new toys to keep your rabbit engaged.

3. Litter Training Your Rabbit:

Did you know that rabbits can be litter trained? Train your rabbit to use a litter box filled with rabbit-safe litter.

Grooming:

Brush your rabbit's fur regularly, especially for long-haired breeds, to prevent matting and reduce shedding. Check their ears for signs of infection or mites, and trim their nails if needed. Introduce grooming gradually, using a gentle brush and positive reinforcement.

4. Properly Holding Them:

Learn the correct way to handle and hold them, supporting their hindquarters to avoid stress or injury. Practice positive reinforcement during training and handling to build trust with your rabbit.

5. Daily Feedings and Regular Cleanings:

Provide a consistent feeding schedule with fresh water. Clean your rabbit's living space regularly, including the litter box, to maintain hygiene and prevent health issues. Monitor your rabbit's weight and adjust their diet accordingly. Regularly check their teeth for signs of malocclusion.

6. Daily Exercise Outside of Their Enclosure (if they have one):

Allow your rabbit supervised time outside their enclosure for exercise and exploration. Ensure the area is safe and free from potential dangers. Create an enriching environment with tunnels, toys, forage...

7. Providing Safe Chew Toys:

Offer appropriate chew toys, such as wooden blocks or untreated wicker, to keep your rabbit's teeth healthy and satisfy their natural chewing instincts. Introduce a variety of textures and shapes to prevent boredom and encourage dental wear.

8. Regular Check-ups with a Veterinarian:

Schedule routine veterinary visits to monitor your rabbit's health, discuss preventive care, and address any concerns promptly. Discuss vaccination options with your veterinarian based on your rabbit's lifestyle and health history. Keep a record of your rabbit's health history and share it with your vet during visits.

9. Spaying or Neutering:

Consider spaying or neutering your rabbit, as it can prevent certain health issues and undesirable behaviors. Consult with your veterinarian for the best timing and advice. Discuss the benefits of the procedure and any potential risks or considerations specific to your rabbit's breed or health status.
