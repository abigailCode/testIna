Rabbit breeds

The ARBA recognizes 49 breeds of rabbit all together, but there are many other breeds out there. These are some of the most popular:

Holland Lop

The Holland Lop is the smallest of the lop breeds and weighs a maximum of 4 lbs. They have lop ears and a short, thick coat.

Netherland Dwarf

The Netherland Dwarf is a tiny breed with a big personality. They weigh just 1 to 2.5 pounds and are full of energy!

Dutch

Dutch rabbits are recognized for their distinctive markings. These charming bunnies have a white blaze on their face and weigh approximately 4 to 5.5 pounds.

Flemish Giant

Known for its impressive size, the Flemish Giant is one of the largest rabbit breeds. These gentle giants can weigh up to 15 pounds!

Angora

The Angora rabbit is known for its small to medium size, upright ears, and long, silky fur. There are different types, including English, French, and Giant Angoras, each with specific coat characteristics. Regular grooming is crucial to prevent matting, and their wool is prized for its softness in the textile industry.

Lionhead

The Lionhead rabbit is a small to medium-sized breed known for its distinctive "mane" of longer fur around the head, resembling a lion's mane.

Rex

The Rex rabbit is known for its distinctive short, dense, and plush coat with a velvety texture. Rex rabbits come in various colors, and their fur gives them a unique appearance.

Californian

The Californian rabbit is a medium to large-sized breed with a white body and distinct dark points on ears, nose, feet, and tail. Known for its commercial body type, bright red eyes, and calm temperament, it was originally bred for meat production and is also kept as a friendly pet.
